Guides

With so many machines to choose from, knowing how to use them safely and effectively is key. These guides break down how to use each machine, highlight which muscles they target, and explain the benefits, making it easier for you to crush your workouts.

Types of Machines

  1. Leg Press
  2. Chest Press
  3. Lat Pulldown
  4. Seated Rows
  5. Shoulder Press
  6. Cable Machines
Leg Press
A strength training machine used to target the muscles of the lower body. The user pushes a weighted platform away from them using their legs while seated, which primarily works the quadriceps, hamstrings, glutes, and calves.
Chest Press
A resistance exercise performed on a machine or with free weights, targeting the pectoral muscles of the chest. The user presses weights away from their chest while lying on a bench or seated, which helps build upper body strength.
Lat Pulldown
A machine exercise designed to strengthen the upper back and latissimus dorsi muscles. The user pulls a weighted bar down towards their chest while seated, engaging the back muscles and improving upper body strength.
Seated Rows
A strength training exercise performed on a machine where the user pulls a weighted handle towards their torso while seated. This exercise targets the back muscles, including the lats, rhomboids, and traps, as well as the biceps.
Shoulder Press
A resistance exercise that targets the deltoid muscles of the shoulders. Performed either standing or seated, the user presses weights upward from shoulder height, which helps build shoulder strength and stability.
Cable Machines
Versatile gym equipment that uses a system of pulleys and adjustable weights to provide resistance for a wide range of exercises. The user can perform various movements by adjusting the handles and cables, allowing for targeted strength training and flexibility exercises.
